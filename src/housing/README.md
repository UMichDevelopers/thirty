# Housing

All freshmen are guaranteed on-campus housing, provided you submit the housing
application by the deadline. You are not required to live in a dorm, but ≈98%
of freshmen do.

A dorm is not guaranteed past your first year unless you are in an MLC that
takes 2nd-year; a majority of 2nd-year students and beyond move off-campus.

Everybody who lives in a dorm is required to have an unlimited dining hall
plan. This is included in the cost of your "room and board".

## Furniture

Your room will come with a basic set of furniture that includes a twin XL bed,
a desk, and a dresser; these will usually be either [type
I](https://kb.housing.umich.edu/hc/en-us/articles/360003071492-Lifespace-II-Modular-Furniture-Information)
or [type
II](https://kb.housing.umich.edu/hc/en-us/articles/360003072952-Building-Block-Furniture-Information).
(The Martha Cook building is an exception.) 

You are welcome to bring whatever else you want as long as it's not
[prohibited](https://kb.housing.umich.edu/hc/en-us/articles/32856504719629-Regulated-and-Prohibited-Items-in-Michigan-Housing).

## Rates and costs

[Housing rates for each room
type](https://housing.umich.edu/undergraduate-rates/] are updated every June.

## Choosing dorms

- Self selection is where you get assigned a random timeslot, which is when
  you'd select your specific dorm room.

- Placement by preference is where you can put down your preferences such as:
  room occupancy/type and central or north campus. Note that your preferences
  are not guaranteed to be met. Hopefully more freshmen who want central campus
  are able to get it this year since Wolverine Village is adding 2,300 beds.

  > [!NOTE]
  > MHousing sets aside a certain number of beds in each dorm for students who
  > opted to be placed by preference. This means you won't be left with all the
  > "bad dorms" after people who did self selection chose their rooms.

- The only way to guarantee yourself a specific dorm is to apply and be
  admitted into a [Michigan Learning Community
  (MLC)](https://lsa.umich.edu/mlc).

MHousing will provide
[accomodations](https://housing.umich.edu/additional-living-options/) to those
who require them, such as for medical or identity reasons. 

Your dorm room is finalized when you sign the housing contract, which typically
occurs in June or July.
